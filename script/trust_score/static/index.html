  1. Learning Phase: Sport-specific images are shown in correct sequence. Your brain memorises the order while we capture a baseline pupil measurement.
  2. Swap Introduction: Two images in the sequence are randomly swapped to create a controlled anomaly.
  3. Test Phase: The modified sequence is displayed again while your pupil size is tracked at high frequency via webcam iris detection.
  4. Relative Comparison (Cohen's d): We compare the mean pupil size at swapped images against the mean at correct images. A medium-or-greater effect size (d ≥ 0.5) indicates the brain detected the anomaly — proving domain expertise.
  5. Liveness Validation: Blink detection and face presence checks ensure a real human is present, not a photo or video replay.
  6. Reaction Time Analysis: The speed of the pupil response at swapped images provides an additional confidence signal — faster reactions indicate stronger recognition.
